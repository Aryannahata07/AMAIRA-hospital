Infertility Assessment


Our specialized care includes:

Couple Workup:

A detailed check-up of both partners to understand possible fertility issues. It includes medical history, lifestyle habits, and necessary tests.

Semen Analysis:

A test to check sperm count, movement, and shape to see if they are healthy enough for pregnancy. It helps diagnose male fertility problems.

Sonography:

An ultrasound scan to check the health of reproductive organs like the uterus and ovaries. It also tracks egg development in women.

Hormonal Testing:

Blood tests to check hormone levels that control ovulation and sperm production. It helps identify any hormonal imbalances affecting fertility.

Genetic Testing:

A test to check if inherited conditions are affecting fertility. It is useful for couples facing repeated miscarriages or genetic disorders.

Infertility Treatment


Our specialized care includes:

Folliculometry:

Folliculometry is an ultrasound-based technique used to track the growth and development of ovarian follicles during a woman’s menstrual cycle. It plays a crucial role in assessing ovulation, monitoring fertility treatments, and guiding assisted reproductive technologies (ART)

ICSI (Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection):

A specialized IVF technique where a single healthy sperm is injected directly into an egg. It is used in cases of severe male infertility.

IUI (Intrauterine Insemination):

A simple procedure where healthy sperm is placed directly into the uterus. It increases the chances of fertilization for couples with minor fertility issues.

IVF (In Vitro Fertilization):

The process of fertilizing eggs and sperm in a lab, then placing the embryo into the uterus. It is helpful for couples facing difficulties in conceiving naturally.

Ovulation Induction with Drugs:

Medicines are given to help women with irregular cycles release eggs regularly. This increases the chances of pregnancy naturally.

Fertility Enhancing Endoscopy


Our specialized care includes:

Hysteroscopy:

A simple procedure to check the inside of the uterus for any problems that may affect fertility. It helps in diagnosing and treating uterine conditions.

Laparoscopy:

A minimally invasive surgery to check and treat fertility-related issues inside the abdomen. It can remove cysts, fibroids, or blockages in tubes.

Endometriosis Surgeries:

A procedure to remove abnormal tissue growing outside the uterus, which causes pain and fertility issues. It helps reduce discomfort and improves chances of pregnancy.

Ovarian Cyst Removal:

Surgery to remove fluid-filled sacs (cysts) from the ovaries while preserving fertility. It helps relieve pain, prevent complications, and restore normal ovarian function.

Ovarian Drilling:

A laparoscopic procedure to treat polycystic ovaries by making small holes in the ovary. It helps restore ovulation and improve fertility in women with PCOS.

Advanced Treatment


Our specialized care includes:

Laser Assisted Hatching (LAH)

A technique that helps embryos hatch from their outer shell, improving implantation chances. It is often recommended for patients with previous failed IVF cycles.

Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT):

A screening method that checks embryos for genetic abnormalities before implantation. It helps in selecting the healthiest embryo for a successful pregnancy.

Sperm DNA Fragmentation Index (DFI) Test:

A test that measures the level of DNA damage in sperm. High fragmentation can affect fertility and increase the chances of miscarriage.

PICSI (Physiological ICSI):

An advanced sperm selection technique used in ICSI treatment. It ensures that only the healthiest and most mature sperm are used for fertilization.

Micro TESE (Microsurgical Testicular Sperm Extraction):

An advanced sperm selection technique used in ICSI treatment. It ensures that only the healthiest and most mature sperm are used for fertilization.

Cology


Our specialized care includes:

Management of Oligozoospermia:

Oligozoospermia means low sperm count, which can reduce fertility. Treatments include medications, lifestyle changes, and advanced sperm retrieval techniques.

Surgical Sperm Retrieval:

Techniques used to extract sperm directly from the testes or epididymis in men with obstructive or non-obstructive azoospermia. It helps in assisted reproduction like IVF and ICSI.

Erectile Dysfunction (ED) Treatment:

ED is the inability to maintain an erection, affecting sexual health. Treatment options include medications, counseling, and advanced therapies like shockwave therapy.

Ejaculation Disorders:

Conditions such as premature, delayed, or retrograde ejaculation can impact fertility and intimacy. Treatments range from medications to behavioral therapy.

Varicocele Treatment:

Varicocele is an enlarged vein in the scrotum that can affect sperm production and quality. It can be treated with minimally invasive surgery to improve fertility.

Fertility Preservation


Our specialized care includes:

Egg Freezing:

Egg freezing is a fertility preservation technique that allows women to store their eggs for future use. This process is beneficial for those who wish to delay childbearing due to career goals, medical conditions , or personal reasons.

Sperm Freezing:

Sperm freezing is a widely used method for preserving male fertility. It is especially useful for individuals undergoing medical treatments like chemotherapy, radiation, or surgeries affecting reproductive organs, as well as for those in high-risk professions

Ovarian Tissue Freezing:

Ovarian tissue freezing is an experimental but promising fertility preservation technique, particularly for young girls and women who cannot undergo ovarian stimulation for egg freezing due to medical conditions like cancer or early menopause.

Embryo Cryopreservation:

Embryo cryopreservation is the process of freezing and storing fertilized embryos for future use. This method is commonly used in IVF treatments, where multiple high-quality embryos are created, and only one or two are implanted immediately.

Fertility Counseling:

Fertility treatments can be physically, emotionally, and financially demanding. Fertility counseling offers professional guidance to individuals and couples navigating reproductive challenges, helping them make informed decisions about their reproductive health.

Third-Party Reproduction


Our specialized care includes:

Surrogacy:

Surrogacy is an arrangement where another woman, known as a surrogate mother, carries a pregnancy on behalf of intended parents who may be unable to conceive or carry a pregnancy to term.

Donor Embryos:

For couples facing severe infertility, where neither partner can provide viable gametes, donor embryos offer a chance to experience pregnancy and childbirth. These embryos may come from anonymous donors or from couples who have undergone IVF and choose to donate their remaining embryos.

Emotional Support Services:

Undergoing fertility treatments can be emotionally taxing, with challenges like stress, anxiety, depression, and relationship strain. Emotional support services play a vital role in helping individuals and couples navigate their fertility journey.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

When should I consider seeing a fertility specialist? ▼
If you’ve been trying to conceive for over a year (or six months if you’re over 35) without success, or if you have irregular periods, PCOS, endometriosis, or a history of miscarriages, it’s time to see a specialist.
What are the first steps in diagnosing infertility? ▼
The initial steps include a detailed medical history, blood tests, ultrasound scans, semen analysis for men, and ovulation tracking for women. Based on the results, further tests or treatments may be recommended.
What is the difference between IVF and IUI? ▼
IUI (Intrauterine Insemination) involves placing sperm directly into the uterus during ovulation, while IVF (In Vitro Fertilization) involves fertilizing eggs in a lab and then transferring the embryo into the uterus. IVF is more advanced and has higher success rates.
What precautions should I take before starting fertility treatments? ▼
Maintain a healthy weight, eat a balanced diet, exercise moderately, quit smoking, limit alcohol, and manage stress. A fertility specialist will provide personalized guidance.
Can age impact my chances of getting pregnant through fertility treatments? ▼
Yes, fertility declines with age. Women in their 20s and early 30s have higher chances of conception, while success rates drop after 35. Egg freezing can be an option for those planning to delay pregnancy.
