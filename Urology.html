Male Urology


Our specialized care for men includes:

Urethral Stricture Treatment:

Urethral stricture refers to the narrowing of the urethra, which is the tube that carries urine from the bladder out of the body. It is a medical condition that can affect both men and women, although it is more common in men.

Male Infertility:

Male infertility refers to the inability of a man to impregnate a woman due to factors related to his reproductive system. It is a common issue and can be caused by various factors.

Prostate Cancer Care:

Prostate cancer is a type of cancer that develops in the prostate gland, which is a small walnut-shaped gland in the male reproductive system. It is one of the most common cancers among men worldwide. The prostate gland produces seminal fluid that nourishes and transports sperm.

Prostate Surgery:

Prostate surgery, also known as prostatectomy, is a surgical procedure performed to remove all or part of the prostate gland. The prostate is a small gland located below the bladder in men, and it surrounds the urethra, the tube that carries urine and semen out of the body.

Erectile Dysfunction Management:

Erectile dysfunction (ED), also known as impotence, is a condition where a man experiences difficulty achieving or maintaining an erection sufficient for sexual activity. It is a common condition that can affect men of all ages, although it is more prevalent in older men.

Female Urology


Our specialized care for women includes:

Female Urinary Incontinence:

Female urinary incontinence is a common condition where a woman experiences involuntary leakage of urine. It can range from occasional small leaks to a complete loss of bladder control. This condition can have a significant impact on a woman's quality of life and may cause embarrassment, social isolation, and emotional distress.

Interstitial Cystitlis:

Interstitial cystitis (IC), also known as bladder pain syndrome (BPS), is a chronic condition characterized by recurring discomfort or pain in the bladder and pelvic region. It is considered a complex disorder with an unknown cause and is more common in women than in men.

Overactive Bladder:

Overactive bladder (OAB) is a medical condition characterized by a frequent and urgent need to urinate. People with overactive bladder often experience sudden and uncontrollable urges to urinate, even when the bladder may not be full. This can lead to an increased frequency of urination, including during the night (nocturia), and sometimes can result in urinary incontinence (involuntary loss of urine).

Vesicovaginal Fistula:

A vesicovaginal fistula (VVF) is an abnormal connection or passageway between the urinary bladder and the vagina. It typically occurs as a result of injury or trauma to the tissues that separate these two structures, leading to the formation of a hole or communication between them. This abnormal connection allows urine to pass from the bladder into the vagina, resulting in urinary incontinence.

Surgery


Our urology department specializes in advanced surgical treatments for various urological conditions.

Ureteric Stones:

Treatment for stones lodged in the ureter that cause pain and block urine flow. Our procedures include non-invasive and minimally invasive options to remove the stones effectively.

Bladder Stones:

Bladder stones are treated using advanced surgical techniques to alleviate discomfort and restore normal bladder function.

Antegrade PCNL:

Percutaneous nephrolithotomy (PCNL) performed through a small incision to remove kidney stones, offering a less invasive solution for complex cases.

ESWL (Extracorporeal Shock Wave Lithotripsy):

A non-invasive procedure that uses sound waves to break kidney or ureteric stones into smaller pieces, allowing them to pass naturally.

PCNL:

A surgical procedure for removing larger or complex kidney stones using a keyhole incision, ensuring effective stone clearance.

Mini PCNL:

A minimally invasive variant of PCNL, performed with smaller instruments, reducing recovery time and complications.

Ultra Mini PCNL:

An even more refined version of PCNL that uses ultra-small instruments for a gentler and quicker recovery.

RIRS (Retrograde Intrarenal Surgery):

A highly advanced and minimally invasive procedure performed with a flexible ureteroscope to treat kidney stones and other conditions without incisions.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What are the common symptoms of kidney stones? ▼
Kidney stones can cause severe pain in the lower back or side, pain while urinating, blood in the urine, nausea, vomiting, and frequent urination. If you experience these symptoms, consult a urologist immediately.
What are the symptoms of prostate cancer, and when should I get screened? ▼
Symptoms may include difficulty urinating, weak urine flow, blood in urine, or pelvic discomfort. If you are over 50 or have a family history of prostate cancer, regular screenings are recommended.
When should I see a urologist for frequent urination? ▼
If you're urinating more than usual, waking up multiple times at night to urinate, or experiencing pain or urgency while urinating, it’s best to consult a urologist. Frequent urination can be a sign of infections, diabetes, bladder issues, or prostate problems.
What treatment options are available for kidney stones? ▼
Treatment depends on the size and location of the stone. Small stones may pass naturally with hydration and pain management. Larger stones may require medications, laser lithotripsy, extracorporeal shock wave lithotripsy (ESWL), or surgery.
What is the treatment for male infertility due to urological issues? ▼
Treatment depends on the cause and may include medication, lifestyle changes, hormone therapy, or surgical procedures like varicocele repair or sperm retrieval techniques for assisted reproduction.
